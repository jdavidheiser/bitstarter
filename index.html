There has been much talk in the media, academic institutions, and government about closing the STEM Skills Gap - there are technical jobs available, but it has proven difficult to connect Science, Technology, Engineering, and Math majors with these positions. STEMployed seeks to help close this gap, by providing a powerful platform which exploits the power of Data Science to identify skills that are in demand, and provide a powerful hub to connect employers with the people who have these skills.

STEMployed is all about skills - tangible abilities with direct relevance to day-to-day tasks. This isn't a social network, it's an interdependent, collaborative network for connecting people based on skills, interests, and needs. We challenge the notion that "it's not what you know, but who you know" that leads to success in business - and we will take even further - by helping you figure out what other people know, and what you need to know.

FAQ

How is this different from other job websites or social networks?
STEMployed focuses heavily on skills, and makes use of that data to put the power in the hands of the users. The old method of searching for a job based on "keywords" is outdated and inefficient. Trying to use generic conversations with your extended professional network to find a job is like finding a needle in a haystack. By moving away from these outdated modalities and in the direction of directly linking people based on the skills they posess and value, we bridge the skills gap by helping you seek out the best people to work with.
How will positions be listed on the website, if they are not traditional job listings?
Positions will be boiled down to lists of skills with a brief text description for employers to describe company culture, benefits, etc. Machine learning algorithms will scan text descriptions from traditional position descriptions and recommend relevant skills to add to a listing.
How else can this data be used?
In a great variety of ways - this is in many ways the most exciting aspect of this project. Not only can we connect job seekers with employers, but we can help close the skills gap in more dramatic fashion. For example, we can identify skills that show up in many job listings, but are not present in many users' skills profiles - this information could be used to decide on which courses should be offered by a University or Massively Online Open Course (MOOC). The same data could help prospective students decide what to study.
What about transparency?
This is a key emphasis of STEMployed - finding the right balance between proprietary information and algorithms, and making useful information available to the public. As an example, if a user is matched with a particular job opening, we want that user to know why he or she was matched with that position - what skills were relevant, what skills might be useful to obtain?
